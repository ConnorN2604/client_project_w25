Academic Resources at UMSI

At the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI), we are committed to supporting our students' academic success by offering a wide array of resources and services.

Our academic advising team provides personalized guidance to help you navigate your curriculum, select courses, and address any academic challenges or questions you may encounter during your studies.

UMSI students also have access to the extensive collections and study spaces within the University Library system, offering both digital and physical resources essential for research and coursework.

Additionally, our specialized tutoring services and the UMSI Writing Center are available to support you in mastering complex subjects and enhancing your written communication skills.

By utilizing these academic resources, UMSI students can thrive in their studies and achieve their educational goals.

This page is a breakdown of the BSI degree at the University of Michigan. Look through to learn more about the degree and its requirements!

What is Information Science?

Information Science at the University of Michigan focuses on understanding and designing how people interact with information, data, and technology. It combines technical skills with a deep understanding of human behavior to address real-world challenges in an increasingly digital world. Students in this major learn to bridge the gap between technology and society by exploring topics such as data analytics, user experience design, and information policy.

Within the major, students can choose between two pathways:

  • Information Analysis: This pathway emphasizes data-driven decision-making and problem-solving. Students learn to collect, analyze, and interpret data to provide insights and solutions for organizations and communities. Key topics include data visualization, machine learning, and database management, preparing students for roles like data analysts and business intelligence specialists.
  • User Experience Design (UX): This pathway focuses on creating intuitive, accessible, and impactful digital experiences. Students study usability, accessibility, and design principles to build technology that meets user needs. This pathway prepares students for roles like UX designers, interaction designers, and product managers.

The Information Science major is ideal for students who want to leverage technology to solve human-centered problems and create meaningful, impactful change in diverse industries.

Dual Degree Policy

BSI students can pursue a dual degree with the College of Science and Engineering; College of Literature Science and the Arts; Ross School of Business; School of Music, Theatre, and Dance; School of Kinesiology; and STAMPS School of Art and Design. The university requires students to earn a minimum of 150 credits for a dual degree.
